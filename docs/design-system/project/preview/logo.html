Construction

Two glyphs, one gesture

Bottle anchors the lower-right (cx=36, base y=58). Feather enters from upper-left at ~45° and its nib lands inside the neck — the act of dipping is captured at the moment of contact.

Feather · spine + vane + 3 barbs
Ink bottle · hand-soap minus nozzle

Composition rules

  • Both glyphs use currentColor. Recolor the parent, the mark recolors with you.
  • Stroke is 2.6 px on the 64-grid. Match the corporate mark's weight — they're meant to sit side by side.
  • Vane has a faint fill-opacity 0.18 tint to give the feather body without competing with the bottle silhouette.
  • Ink-level line on the bottle uses stroke-opacity 0.55 — visual interest without adding clutter at favicon size.
  • Clear space = one ink-bottle width (~14px on the 64-grid).
  • Pairs with Inter 700 for the wordmark "Notes" (one weight lighter than the corporate "Daydream" — so the parent brand stays dominant in lockups).

When to flag this

Working sketch. Variants worth trying — feather as outline only (no vane fill), bottle with a wax-seal cap instead of flat lid, or feather entering vertically (top-down dip) for a calmer composition. Tell me which way to push.

Construction

Concentric, on a single center

Sun ring + 8 rays centered at (32, 32). Moon crescent nested in the same center, opening leftward so its bulge fills the right interior. The moon never touches the sun's ring — clear space all around.

Sun · ring + 8 rays
Moon · crescent, nested

Composition rules

  • Both shapes use currentColor. Recolor the parent, the mark recolors with you.
  • Sun ring stroke is 2.6 px on the 64-grid. If you need a heavier mark, scale the SVG — don't bump the stroke.
  • Moon never touches the sun's interior ring — leaves ~5px of breathing room at the smallest sizes.
  • Glyph is centered on (32, 32). Drop it into any square, no manual alignment needed.
  • Clear space around the full mark = one ray length (~5px on the 64-grid).
  • Pairs with Inter 800 at -0.02em tracking for the wordmark.

When to flag this

Still a working sketch. Variants worth trying if this isn't the one — moon as a stroked outline instead of a solid fill, fewer rays (4 cardinals only), or the moon shifted slightly off-center for asymmetric tension. Tell me which way to push and I'll iterate.
